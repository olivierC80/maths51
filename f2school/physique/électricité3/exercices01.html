Rappel de Cours : Conditions de passage

1) Interface entre deux diélectriques ($\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2$), $\sigma_l=0$.

(Schéma interface diélectriques $\vec{D}, \vec{E}$)

• Composante normale de $\vec{D}$ : $(\vec{D}_2 - \vec{D}_1) \cdot \vec{n}_{12} = \sigma_l = 0 \implies$

$$ D_{2n} = D_{1n} $$

• Composante tangentielle de $\vec{E}$ : $(\vec{E}_2 - \vec{E}_1) \times \vec{n}_{12} = \vec{0} \implies$

$$ \vec{E}_{2t} = \vec{E}_{1t} $$

On en déduit :

$$ \frac{\vec{D}_{1t}}{\epsilon_1} = \frac{\vec{D}_{2t}}{\epsilon_2} $$

2) Interface entre deux milieux magnétiques ($\mu_1, \mu_2$), $\vec{j}_s=\vec{0}$.

(Schéma interface magnétiques $\vec{B}, \vec{H}$)

• Composante normale de $\vec{B}$ : $(\vec{B}_2 - \vec{B}_1) \cdot \vec{n}_{12} = 0 \implies$

$$ B_{2n} = B_{1n} $$

• Composante tangentielle de $\vec{H}$ : $(\vec{H}_2 - \vec{H}_1) \times \vec{n}_{12} = \vec{j}_s = \vec{0} \implies$

$$ \vec{H}_{2t} = \vec{H}_{1t} $$

On en déduit :

$$ \frac{\vec{B}_{1t}}{\mu_1} = \frac{\vec{B}_{2t}}{\mu_2} $$

I - Lame diélectrique L.H.I.

Basé sur l'Exercice 1 du TD.

1) Figure 1.a : $\vec{E}_0 = E_0 \vec{e}_x$ (normal), $\vec{D}_0 = \epsilon_0 \vec{E}_0$.

a) Champs internes $\vec{D}_{int}, \vec{E}_{int}, \vec{E}_p$.

Continuité: $D_{int, x} = D_{0, x} = \epsilon_0 E_0$ ; $E_{int, y} = E_{0, y} = 0$, $E_{int, z} = E_{0, z} = 0$.

$$ \vec{D}_{int} = \epsilon_0 E_0 \vec{e}_x $$
$$ \vec{E}_{int} = \frac{\vec{D}_{int}}{\epsilon} = \frac{\epsilon_0 E_0}{\epsilon} \vec{e}_x $$
$$ \vec{E}_{int} = \frac{\epsilon_0}{\epsilon} E_0 \vec{e}_x $$
$$ \vec{E}_p = \vec{E}_{int} - \vec{E}_0 = \left( \frac{\epsilon_0}{\epsilon} - 1 \right) E_0 \vec{e}_x = \frac{\epsilon_0 - \epsilon}{\epsilon} E_0 \vec{e}_x $$
$$ \vec{E}_p = \frac{\epsilon_0 - \epsilon}{\epsilon} E_0 \vec{e}_x $$

b) Polarisation $\vec{P}$ et charges liées.

$$ \vec{P} = (\epsilon - \epsilon_0) \vec{E}_{int} = (\epsilon - \epsilon_0) \frac{\epsilon_0}{\epsilon} E_0 \vec{e}_x $$
$$ \vec{P} = \frac{\epsilon_0 (\epsilon - \epsilon_0)}{\epsilon} E_0 \vec{e}_x $$

$\rho_p = -\text{div} \vec{P} = 0$ (car $\vec{P}$ uniforme).

$\sigma_p = \vec{P} \cdot \vec{n}$ ($\vec{n}$ sortant).

Face 1 ($x=-a$, $\vec{n}=-\vec{e}_x$): $\sigma_{p, face1} = -P_x$.

Face 2 ($x=+a$, $\vec{n}=+\vec{e}_x$): $\sigma_{p, face2} = P_x$.

$$ \sigma_{p, face1} = - \frac{\epsilon_0 (\epsilon - \epsilon_0)}{\epsilon} E_0 \quad ; \quad \sigma_{p, face2} = \frac{\epsilon_0 (\epsilon - \epsilon_0)}{\epsilon} E_0 $$
(Schéma Fig 1a avec $\sigma_p$)

2) Figure 1.b : $\vec{E}_0 = E_0 \vec{e}_y$ (parallèle).

a) Champs internes $\vec{D}_{int}, \vec{E}_{int}, \vec{E}_p$.

Continuité: $D_{int, x} = D_{0, x} = 0$ ; $E_{int, y} = E_{0, y} = E_0$, $E_{int, z} = E_{0, z} = 0$.

$$ \vec{E}_{int} = E_0 \vec{e}_y $$
$$ \vec{D}_{int} = \epsilon \vec{E}_{int} = \epsilon E_0 \vec{e}_y $$
$$ \vec{D}_{int} = \epsilon E_0 \vec{e}_y $$
$$ \vec{E}_p = \vec{E}_{int} - \vec{E}_0 = \vec{0} $$
$$ \vec{E}_p = \vec{0} $$

b) Polarisation $\vec{P}$ et charges liées.

$$ \vec{P} = (\epsilon - \epsilon_0) \vec{E}_{int} = (\epsilon - \epsilon_0) E_0 \vec{e}_y $$
$$ \vec{P} = (\epsilon - \epsilon_0) E_0 \vec{e}_y $$

$\rho_p = 0$ ; $\sigma_p = \vec{P} \cdot (\pm \vec{e}_x) = 0$.

$$ \rho_p = 0 \quad ; \quad \sigma_p = 0 $$

3) Figure 1.c : $\vec{E}_0 = E_0 \cos\alpha \vec{e}_x + E_0 \sin\alpha \vec{e}_y$.

a) Champs internes $\vec{D}_{int}, \vec{E}_{int}, \vec{E}_p$.

Continuité: $D_{int, x} = D_{0, x} = \epsilon_0 E_0 \cos\alpha$ ; $E_{int, y} = E_{0, y} = E_0 \sin\alpha$.

$$ E_{int, x} = \frac{D_{int, x}}{\epsilon} = \frac{\epsilon_0 E_0 \cos\alpha}{\epsilon} $$ $$ D_{int, y} = \epsilon E_{int, y} = \epsilon E_0 \sin\alpha $$
$$ \vec{E}_{int} = E_0 \left( \frac{\epsilon_0}{\epsilon} \cos\alpha \vec{e}_x + \sin\alpha \vec{e}_y \right) $$
$$ \vec{D}_{int} = E_0 \left( \epsilon_0 \cos\alpha \vec{e}_x + \epsilon \sin\alpha \vec{e}_y \right) $$
$$ \vec{E}_p = \vec{E}_{int} - \vec{E}_0 = E_0 \left( (\frac{\epsilon_0}{\epsilon} - 1) \cos\alpha \vec{e}_x \right) = \frac{\epsilon_0 - \epsilon}{\epsilon} E_0 \cos\alpha \vec{e}_x $$
$$ \vec{E}_p = \frac{\epsilon_0 - \epsilon}{\epsilon} E_0 \cos\alpha \vec{e}_x $$

b) Polarisation $\vec{P}$ et charges liées.

$$ \vec{P} = (\epsilon - \epsilon_0) \vec{E}_{int} = (\epsilon - \epsilon_0) E_0 \left( \frac{\epsilon_0}{\epsilon} \cos\alpha \vec{e}_x + \sin\alpha \vec{e}_y \right) $$
$$ \vec{P} = E_0 \left( \frac{\epsilon_0(\epsilon - \epsilon_0)}{\epsilon} \cos\alpha \vec{e}_x + (\epsilon - \epsilon_0) \sin\alpha \vec{e}_y \right) $$

$\rho_p = 0$.

$\sigma_{p, face1} = -P_x = - E_0 \frac{\epsilon_0(\epsilon - \epsilon_0)}{\epsilon} \cos\alpha$.

$\sigma_{p, face2} = P_x = E_0 \frac{\epsilon_0(\epsilon - \epsilon_0)}{\epsilon} \cos\alpha$.

$$ \rho_p = 0 \quad ; \quad \sigma_{p, face2} = - \sigma_{p, face1} = E_0 \frac{\epsilon_0(\epsilon - \epsilon_0)}{\epsilon} \cos\alpha $$

2) Angle $\beta$ de $\vec{P}$ avec Ox pour Figure 1.c.

$$ \tan \beta = \frac{P_y}{P_x} = \frac{E_0 (\epsilon - \epsilon_0) \sin\alpha}{E_0 \frac{\epsilon_0(\epsilon - \epsilon_0)}{\epsilon} \cos\alpha} = \frac{\epsilon}{\epsilon_0} \tan\alpha $$
$$ \tan \beta = \epsilon_r \tan \alpha $$

Comparaison : Comme $\epsilon_r > 1$, $\tan\beta > \tan\alpha$. Si $\alpha \in [0, \pi/2]$, alors $\beta > \alpha$.

3) Schémas des charges et vecteurs.

• Figure 1.b: Pas de charges. $\vec{E}_{int}=\vec{E}_0$, $\vec{P} // \vec{E}_0$, $\vec{E}_p=\vec{0}$.

(Schéma Fig 1.b avec vecteurs)

• Figure 1.c: Charges $\sigma_p$ sur les faces. $\vec{E}_{int}, \vec{P}$ ont angle $\beta > \alpha$. $\vec{E}_p // -\vec{e}_x$.

(Schéma Fig 1.c avec charges et vecteurs)

II - Lame magnétique L.H.I.

Basé sur l'Exercice II du TD. Normale $\vec{e}_y$. $\vec{B}_0 = B_{0x} \vec{e}_x + B_{0y} \vec{e}_y$.

1) a) Champs $\vec{B}_{int}$ et $\vec{H}_{int}$.

Continuité: $B_{int, y} = B_{0y}$ ; $H_{int, x} = H_{0, x} = B_{0x}/\mu_0$.

$\vec{B}_{int} = \mu \vec{H}_{int} \implies B_{int, x} = \mu H_{int, x} = (\mu/\mu_0) B_{0x}$ et $H_{int, y} = B_{int, y} / \mu = B_{0y}/\mu$.

$$ \vec{H}_{int} = \frac{B_{0x}}{\mu_0} \vec{e}_x + \frac{B_{0y}}{\mu} \vec{e}_y $$
$$ \vec{B}_{int} = \frac{\mu}{\mu_0} B_{0x} \vec{e}_x + B_{0y} \vec{e}_y $$

1) b) Aimantation $\vec{M}$.

$$ \vec{M} = \chi_m \vec{H}_{int} = \chi_m \left( \frac{B_{0x}}{\mu_0} \vec{e}_x + \frac{B_{0y}}{\mu} \vec{e}_y \right) $$
$$ \vec{M} = \frac{\chi_m}{\mu_0} B_{0x} \vec{e}_x + \frac{\chi_m}{\mu} B_{0y} \vec{e}_y = \frac{\chi_m}{\mu_0} B_{0x} \vec{e}_x + \frac{\chi_m}{\mu_0(1+\chi_m)} B_{0y} \vec{e}_y $$

1) c) Relation entre $\beta$ (angle $\vec{M}$/Ox) et $\alpha$ (angle $\vec{B}_0$/Ox).

$$ \tan \beta = \frac{M_y}{M_x} = \frac{\chi_m B_{0y} / \mu}{\chi_m B_{0x} / \mu_0} = \frac{\mu_0}{\mu} \frac{B_{0y}}{B_{0x}} $$ $$ \tan \alpha = \frac{B_{0y}}{B_{0x}} $$
$$ \tan \beta = \frac{\mu_0}{\mu} \tan \alpha = \frac{1}{1+\chi_m} \tan \alpha $$

Comparaison :

• Para ($\chi_m > 0$): $\mu > \mu_0 \implies \tan \beta < \tan \alpha \implies \beta < \alpha$.

• Dia ($\chi_m < 0$): $\mu < \mu_0 \implies \tan \beta > \tan \alpha \implies \beta > \alpha$.

2) a) Cas particuliers.

• Cas $\alpha = 0$ ($\vec{B}_0 = B_{0x} \vec{e}_x$, tangentiel): $B_{0y}=0$.

$$ \vec{H}_{int} = \frac{B_{0x}}{\mu_0} \vec{e}_x \quad ; \quad \vec{B}_{int} = \frac{\mu}{\mu_0} B_{0x} \vec{e}_x \quad ; \quad \vec{M} = \frac{\chi_m}{\mu_0} B_{0x} \vec{e}_x $$

• Cas $\alpha = \pi/2$ ($\vec{B}_0 = B_{0y} \vec{e}_y$, normal): $B_{0x}=0$.

$$ \vec{H}_{int} = \frac{B_{0y}}{\mu} \vec{e}_y \quad ; \quad \vec{B}_{int} = B_{0y} \vec{e}_y \quad ; \quad \vec{M} = \frac{\chi_m}{\mu} B_{0y} \vec{e}_y $$

2) b) Représentation des orientations.

• Cas $\alpha = 0$ ($\vec{B}_0 // Ox$):

Para: $\vec{M}//\vec{B}_0$, $B_{int}>B_0$. Dia: $\vec{M}$ opposé $\vec{B}_0$, $B_{int}

(Schéma B0 // Ox, Para & Dia)

• Cas $\alpha = \pi/2$ ($\vec{B}_0 // Oy$):

Para: $\vec{M}//\vec{B}_0$, $\vec{B}_{int}=\vec{B}_0$. Dia: $\vec{M}$ opposé $\vec{B}_0$, $\vec{B}_{int}=\vec{B}_0$.

(Schéma B0 // Oy, Para & Dia)

3) Densités de courants d'aimantation.

$\vec{j}_m = \text{rot}\vec{M} = \vec{0}$.

$\vec{k}_m = \vec{M} \times \vec{n}$. Faces $y=\pm e/2$.

Face 2 ($y=+e/2, \vec{n}=+\vec{e}_y$): $\vec{k}_{m, face2} = \vec{M} \times (+\vec{e}_y) = -M_x \vec{e}_z$.
